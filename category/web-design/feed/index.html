The Most Simple WordPress Backup - For FREE!

Tired of paying for backups for your WordPress site? Looking for the most simple WordPress backup solution, for FREE? Luckily all you need is one plugin that we describe how to use below. Let’s get started!

Why Even Backup Your Site?

Regularly backing up your website is essential for several critical reasons. Firstly, it serves as a safeguard against data loss caused by factors such as server failures, hardware issues, human errors, or cyber attacks. In the event of a security breach or malware attack, a recent backup allows you to quickly restore your website to a secure state. Human errors, such as accidental deletions or overwrites of important files, can be rectified by reverting to a previous backup.

Website administrators may also encounter compatibility issues after software or plugin updates. In such cases, having a backup enables a smooth rollback to a previous working version while issues are addressed. Additionally, server failures or crashes can lead to potential data loss, making backups crucial for quick recovery.

For businesses and e-commerce sites, downtime can result in financial losses, making backups a crucial component of business continuity. Compliance with industry and regional data retention requirements is another reason to prioritize regular backups.

Storing backups securely, preferably in an offsite location or on a separate server, ensures their availability when needed. Automated backup solutions streamline the process and contribute to the peace of mind that comes with knowing your website data is protected and recoverable. In summary, regular website backups are a fundamental aspect of sound website management, offering protection against a range of potential issues that could otherwise compromise data integrity and online presence.

How to Backup Your Site For Free

Here at Davis Digital Consulting, we love to use WPvivid as a cost effective method for creating backups on client WordPress sites as well as our own. WPvivid stands as an exceptional WordPress backup solution, combining user-friendly simplicity with powerful features. Its intuitive interface allows effortless backup creation, download, and scheduled automation, making it a go-to choice for website and agency owners alike. With reliable performance and flexible storage options, WPvivid ensures seamless data protection, earning acclaim for its effectiveness in WordPress backup solutions.
 

To back up your WordPress website for free using the WPvivid plugin, follow these steps:

 

Install and Activate WPvivid Plugin:

 
Go to your WordPress dashboard.
  1. Navigate to “Plugins” > “Add New.”
  2. Search for “WPvivid” and click “Install Now” next to the WPvivid Backup Plugin.
  3. Activate the plugin.
 

Access WPvivid Backup Settings:

 

After activation, go to “WPvivid” in your WordPress plugin dashboard.

 

Configure Backup Settings:

 
In the WPvivid settings, navigate to the “Settings” tab.
 

Configure backup settings such as backup storage location, backup frequency, and retention policy according to your preferences.

 

Create a Backup:

 
Go to the “Backups” tab in the WPvivid settings.
Click on the “Create New Backup” button.

Wait for the backup process to complete. The time it takes depends on the size of your website.

Download the Backup:

 
Once the backup is created, you’ll see it listed in the “Backups” tab.

Click on the “Download” button next to the backup you want to save locally.

 

Verify the Backup:

 

To ensure the backup is valid, you can restore it on a staging environment or a local server for testing.

 

Optional: Set up Remote Storage (Cloud):

 

WPvivid supports remote storage options like Google Drive, Dropbox, and others. You can set up remote storage in the “Settings” tab if you want to store your backups offsite.

Automate Scheduled Backups (Optional):

 

If you want to automate the backup process, you can set up scheduled backups in the “Settings” tab under the “Automation” section.

 
Remember that while WPvivid offers a free version with essential backup features, there may be limitations compared to premium versions. Regularly check for updates and ensure compatibility with your WordPress version.
 
 
Always store backups in a secure location, and consider offsite storage for added security. Additionally, test your backups periodically to ensure they can be successfully restored in case of a website emergency.
